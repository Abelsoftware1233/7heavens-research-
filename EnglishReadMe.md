‚ÄãOverview of the Heavens
‚ÄãIn Islamic theology, the visible universe (with all its stars and galaxies) represents merely the First Heaven (Sama' ad-Dunya), adorned with lamps (stars). Beyond this physical boundary lie the successive spiritual layers that the soul encounters during Al Barzakh (the transitional state after death) and during the Mi'raj (the Night Journey).
‚ÄãThis model links these metaphysical thresholds to specific cosmic milestones to contextualize their immense scale for the human mind.
‚ÄãComprehensive Details per Heaven Level
‚Äã1. The Threshold of the Moon (First Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: ~380,000 km
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: Earth-Moon orbital radius
‚ÄãDescription: The starting point of the heavenly journey. In classical descriptions, this represents the direct boundary of Earth's spiritual dome. It marks the disconnection from earthly ties, where the soul begins its ascent.
‚Äã2. The Solar Threshold (Second Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 150 million km
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: 1 Astronomical Unit (AU) / Earth-Sun distance
‚ÄãDescription: The sphere illuminated by the central star of our solar system. Symbolically, this represents the transition from local planetary boundaries to the deeper matrix of the solar system, leaving behind the blinding light of the physical world.
‚Äã3. The Local Interstellar Zone (Third Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 3.26 Light-years (equal to exactly 1 Parsec)
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: The immediate stellar neighborhood / edge of the Oort Cloud
‚ÄãDescription: The threshold where our Sun shrinks to become just one of many stars. This represents entering the true interstellar medium, where the influence of our own sun gives way to the wider galactic neighborhood.
‚Äã4. The Galactic Core (Fourth Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 8.5 Kiloparsec (~27,700 Light-years)
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: Distance from the Sun to Sagittarius A* (Center of the Milky Way)
‚ÄãDescription: The core that anchors our entire galaxy. In a spiritual sense, this corresponds to transcending individual solar systems and understanding the collective macrostructure of billions of stars rotating around a central point.
‚Äã5. The Galactic Neighborhood (Fifth Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 10 million Light-years
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: Diameter of the Local Group (Milky Way, Andromeda, Triangulum, and satellite galaxies)
‚ÄãDescription: The outer boundary of our galaxy cluster. This level symbolizes an expansion that reaches far beyond our own galaxy, into the vast cosmic voids that separate the various "island universes."
‚Äã6. The Network of Superclusters (Sixth Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 100 million Light-years
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: The scale of the Virgo Supercluster / Laniakea filaments
‚ÄãDescription: A sphere encompassing thousands of galaxy groups, structured into gigantic cosmic threads (filaments). This layer visualizes the cosmic web, in which entire galaxies are reduced to nothing more than dust particles.
‚Äã7. The Horizon of the Observable Universe (Seventh Level)
‚ÄãCosmic Distance: 13.8 billion Light-years
‚ÄãAstronomical Equivalent: Total age and radius of the cosmic horizon
‚ÄãDescription: The extreme edge of physical creation (Sama' ad-Dunya). Beyond this threshold lies the metaphysical boundary that separates physical matter from the higher spiritual dimensions, leading to the Sidrat al-Muntaha (the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary), the Kursi (the Footstool), and ultimately the Arsh (the Universal Throne).

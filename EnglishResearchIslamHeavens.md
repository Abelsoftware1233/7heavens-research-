The Cosmic Odyssey: From the Physical Realm to the Divine Threshold
I. Introduction: The Universe as a Book of Signs
In Islamic cosmology, the universe is not a random collection of matter, but a meticulously designed structure. The Quran repeatedly refers to seven heavens (as-samawat as-sab') as a testament to the Creator's perfection. While modern science maps the "first heaven" using telescopes and mathematics, the Islamic tradition provides the framework for what lies beyond.
II. The First Heaven (As-Sama' ad-Dunya): The Physical Cosmos
The first heaven encompasses everything we can perceive with our senses and instruments. It is the heaven described as being "adorned" with stars and luminous lamps.  
The Scale of Our World:
Our immediate vicinity: The distance from Earth to the Moon is approximately 380,000 km, and the Sun is located about 150 million km away.  
Local space: The stars surrounding our sun are measured on a scale of approximately 3.26 light-years (1 parsec).  
The Milky Way: Our galaxy is a vast spiral structure. The sun is situated about 8.5 kiloparsecs (roughly 27,000 light-years) from the galactic center.  
Andromeda (M31) – Our Great Neighbor:
Andromeda is a massive spiral galaxy located about 2.5 million light-years away. It is larger than the Milky Way and contains an estimated 1 trillion stars. Astronomically, it is our "partner" in the Local Group. In about 4.5 billion years, the Milky Way and Andromeda will collide and merge into one. To the believer, this "cosmic dance" is not chaos, but a movement guided by Allah—a sign (Aya) of His control over physical laws.  
Large-scale structures: The observable universe stretches over 13.8 billion light-years (the age of the universe in light-years). We observe galaxy groups spanning 100 million light-years in width. This is the absolute limit of what we can perceive within the "first heaven."  
III. The Higher Heavens (2 through 6): Spiritual Dimensions
Beyond the edges of the observable universe (the 13.8 billion light-year mark), physical science reaches its limit, and the spiritual reality of the higher heavens begins.
Transition to the Unseen: While the first heaven is governed by laws of gravity and the speed of light, heavens 2 through 6 are described as spheres of increasing spiritual refinement.  
Abodes: Islamic tradition, particularly the accounts of the Isra and Mi'raj (the Night Journey of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ), describes these heavens as the domains of angels and previous prophets. Each heaven acts as a "gate," representing boundaries between different levels of existence.  
IV. The Seventh Heaven (As-Sama' as-Sabi'ah): The Pinnacle
The seventh heaven is the highest layer of creation, serving as the threshold to the Creator's presence.
Bayt al-Ma'mur: Located in the seventh heaven is the "Much-Frequented House." This is to the heavens what the Ka'aba is to the Earth. Every day, 70,000 angels enter this place to worship Allah and never return, illustrating the infinite multitude of angels in God's creation.  
The Meeting: It is in this realm that the Prophet ﷺ met Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham), leaning against the Bayt al-Ma'mur. This marks the seventh heaven as the place of the "forefathers" of faith and the absolute summit of created reality.  
Beyond Space: In the seventh heaven, astronomical measurements (such as kiloparsecs or millions of light-years) lose all meaning. Here, creation is no longer subject to the expansion of the universe, but to the Divine Will and the proximity of the Throne (‘Arsh).  
V. Synthesis: A Unified Reality
The data regarding 380,000 km distances, 100 million light-year wide galaxy groups, and the 13.8 billion-year-old cosmos are not separate from the seven heavens; they are the "outer shell".  
The Islamic View on Science: Islam encourages humanity to study the universe (as in Surah Al-Mulk 67:3: "Look once more: do you see any flaw?"). By studying Andromeda, galaxies, and cosmic structures, we admire the craftsmanship of the First Heaven.  
The Spiritual Conclusion: The seven heavens form a hierarchy of dependence. Earth depends on the Sun, the Sun on the Milky Way, the Milky Way on the First Heaven, and the entire First Heaven is, in its totality, only a fraction of the grandeur of the higher heavens, which in turn depend on the Absolute Power of Allah.
